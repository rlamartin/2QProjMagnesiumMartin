Vaccines (Definition and Examples)

Vaccines are substances that are used in order to protect people from diseases. They do their jobs by entering a person’s body through the nose, mouth, and an injection. The vaccines contain weakened versions of the original viruses. They are not strong enough to be a threat, but your body still solves this problem through the creation of antibodies. This means that when you are infected with the actual virus, your body is prepared to fight back against it.

According to the CDC (2021), some side effects of this vaccine are:

  • Pain
  • Redness
  • Tiredness
  • Headache
  • Chills
  • Fever
  • Nausea
