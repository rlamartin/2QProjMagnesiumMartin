Sexual Violence

With the onset of the pandemic, Online Sexual Abuse and Exploitation of Children (OSAEC) has only worsened. From March to May of 2020 alone, there has been a 264.6 per cent (202,605 reports) increase of OSAEC cases within the country. This is extreme compared to the 76,561 cases within the same time period last 2019. There have also been reports of harassment of female harassment by officials in exchange for passage through checkpoints.

Unemployment

Before the pandemic affected the Philippines, its unemployment rate was at around 5%. However, this all changed when Covid-19 attacked. Since many people were forced to be inside their homes during lockdown, a lot of people lost their jobs. This led to 4.5 million Filipinos losing their jobs in 2020 and the unemployment rate increasing up to 17.6 per cent in April 2020. Fortunately, this number decreased to 8.7 per cent or 3.8 million jobless in October of 2020. Comparing this to October of 2019, it was only 4.6 per cent or 2 million jobless.

Deaths

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), there have been 2,839,111 cases of Covid-19 as of December 29 in the Philippines. 51,213 or 1.8% of the total amount of people have died due to this virus which means that 2,787,988 citizens have recovered from this disease. Based on the graph that can be seen. It is notable that the number of deaths within the country slowly increased with it beginning on March 29 of 2020. July 13 signified a turning point as the number of deaths significantly increased with weekly deaths being less than 100 to suddenly exceeding 200. The weekly number of deaths slowly decreased with a small peak on January 11 of 2021, until April 5 of 2021. 1,321 deaths occurred during that week and the heightened amount of the loss of lives seemed to be constant until November 15, where the number of deaths seemed to decrease until now.
